Problem

  • 70% of musicians experience pain or injury.

  • “Musicians suffer for their Art, but unnecessary suffering inhibits Art” Dr. Gearóid Ó Conchubhair

    • Posture Analysis
    • Seat Angle Tests
    • Seat Pressure Testing

    • Musicians from all seated portions of orchestra were observed while using existing chairs, followed by development rigs.

    • A test rig with adjustable seat plates was used to determine optimal seat angles across the the range of musicians.

    • Seat pressure testing was used to determine the effect of various seat angles on posture and weight transfer of musicians.

Principles

earo has been designed to provide a real benefit to the well being of musicians. The principles behind earo promote balanced seated postures, therefore reducing the potential for injuries in both practice and performance.

The 3 key principles are as follows:

  • Elevation
  • Posture
  • Rotation

  • earo provides an elevated seat height which enables relaxed breathing, by raising the pelvis above the line of the knees.

  • earo allows for an open posture when seated, this in turn allows for a more dynamic attitude when seating during performance.

  • earo enables forward pelvic rotation when seated, which combined with the other elements allows for a grounded stance.

Solution

The earó chair sets a new standard in seating for musicians and provides the most ideal conditions for excellent performance. It accomadates the five main instrument groupings of the seated members of the orchestra: violin, viola, cello, wood wind, brass wind while also providing for the practical handling and staging conditions of the auditorium.
